Currently Playing:

Overwatch 2 Review

The highly anticipated sequel to Blizzard's 2014 release Overwatch is finally here! With a rocky launch week, excited players were forced into queues of 30,000 or more players. That being said, once I was able to start playing the game, I was instantly sucked back into the Overwatch universe. Fast-paced, exciting gameplay reminded me and many other players why they picked up the game in the first place. The new user interface is satisfying to use and makes the game feel much more modern than the original. Something that I and a lot of other returning players aren't as stoked about is the new Battle Pass. With the sequel being free-to-play, players are forced to purchase in-game skins if they want the new items and goodies.

While this new change to the game will take players a while to get used to, it's not a big surprise that Blizzard chose to go this route, seeing how other video games like Fortnite and Valorant have had amazing success with a battle pass in their games. The game introduces three new characters since Overwatch: tank Junker Queen, DPS Sojourn, and support Kiriko. As a support main, I was ecstatic about the release of Kiriko, and she did not dissapoint. Her movements and abilities are some of the most satisfying I've played in Overwatch so far. Don't worry, though, I'm not giving up on my girl Mercy quite yet!

Overall, Overwatch 2 is a nice upgrade to the original game, but the new battle pass is going to take some getting used to.

On the backburner:

Splatoon 3 Review

Splatoon was a release from Nintendo that took the world by storm. A new take on the classic FPS game, Splatoon was fast-paced, fun, and brought in new creative elements that made the game so hard to put down. With the recent release of Splatoon 3, there is even more to be excited about. The classic Splatoon gameplay is still there, but the third game in the series brings even more to the game. Something that all players enjoy are the monthly Splatfests, where players pick teams and battle to seee whose team wins. In Splatoon 3, Nintendo made these even bigger than previous games, with three teams to choose from instead of two, the Splatfests are even more exciting.

Being the third game in a series, the game is still relatively similar to the first two games. I would have liked to have more variety in gameplay than we get, but nevertheless, Splatoon is still such a fun experience, and I don't regret buying the 3rd game. The gameplay is just as exciting as the former games. The bright colors, fun music, and new single player story mode makes Splatoon 3 worth picking up! Let's hope Nintendo keeps rolling out updates with even more ways to customize your character and weapons.
